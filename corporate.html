Marketing

Context of campaign and promotional activities

CONTEXT
The marketing campaign is about the brand Chameleon, a children’s board game that is both educational and fun. Even though it is a children’s board game, our campaign is focused to appeal both children as well as the parents of the children, since they are eventually the people buying the product.

To reach this audience, we wanted to use Instagram and Facebook. During our research for the brand, information was gathered from interviews with our target group. During those interviews, our target group mentioned that they mostly use Instagram as a social media platform, for fun, but also as a place where they can find useful advice and products for their children. Moreover, Facebook is a platform that is used widely by our target audience.

THE MESSAGE
The main message of our marketing campaign is to convey that playing Chameleon the Game is an educational and entertaining game for kids, that can be played with the whole family. Playing board games is a fun way of connecting with each other.
By presenting examples of the game cards, and emphasizing the brand values, we want to express that Chameleon the Game provides a unique and engaging experience that encourages learning and development for children.

The emphasis of the message is also to highlight our unique selling point, the monthly subscription on game cards. This enables parents to keep the game fun and fresh for their children, by having new playing cards every month.

In conclusion, the main intention is to convince parents that Chameleon the Game offers a simple and convenient approach to amuse their children month after month, while also fostering their development.

PROMOTIONAL ACTIVITIES

To be able to spread this message, we posted a variety of content on our social media profiles.
Our Instagram account
Our Facebook account

The campaign started by introducing Camo the Chameleon, the brand’s mascot. With this mascot we wanted to create a lovable figure, that would make it easier for our followers to understand our values and message.







We used Camo to emphasize the brand’s values. We made adjusted illustrations of Camo, the chameleon that is visible on our logo. These illustrations were a way of making our values visible. The idea was that Camo is the brand but introduces himself as a character. This way it is not just: “one of our values is introducing curiosity” But more: “ I am a curious chameleon”. This way, we wanted to have people connect more to the brand, by expressing our values in a visual appealing way.







Apart from posting our values through Camo the Chameleon, we also posted an Instagram reel on our page. By posting the reel, we wanted to make more engaging content for our followers. The reel included an image with actual children, with a text that is a message directed to the parents. The message emphasizes the company’s subscription service, which is our unique selling point.







Moreover, we also experimented with two different ways of sharing the same content. We wanted to share examples of our question and task cards. One of the posts, was just an illustration of a task card, the second post was a picture of someone holding a question card. We wanted to find out which post would have more likes, comments, and reach.







Apart from the posts on our page, we also used Instagram stories to interact with our followers. We used stories to create involvement and a feeling of community among the audience. Polls were used to get input from our consumers and followers, which we could use to further develop the game and our marketing plan. We used this interactive strategy for raising brand recognition, but it also for creating a channel for continuing dialog between the brand and the intended market.







Lastly, we also posted hashtags with every post. We did this to reach even more people, that are not following us. Our tactic was to use very broad hashtags, so it we could reach as much people as possible.


THE OBJECTIVES

Reach objective: ‘On the 14th of April, we want to have reached at least 200 people on Instagram and Facebook.,’
On both Instagram and Facebook, it is possible to see the reached people. This means that you can find out how many people saw the content on your page. We feel like 200 is a doable goal for a brand that is just starting.

Affect objective: ‘With our content, we want the audience to feel happy, therefore we want to have a average of 65 likes on our posts on the 14th of April.’
We want to radiate our value fun, and therefore we want our content to be colourful and fun. To find out if our followers like our content, we will measure the amount of likes we get.

Response objective: ‘At the 14th of April, we want that 20 people have clicked on the link to our website, that is shared on our Instagram page.’
We want to make our followers curious to our brand and product, therefore we want to know how many people actually take that extra step to open the site.

Learning Points

Throughout the duration of our marketing campaign, we discovered several important lessons that could improve our strategy and help us connect with our target audience more effectively.

Asking questions: Asking questions in our posts and stories proved to be one of the most successful methods of creating interactivity, because it was a really engaging approach for our followers. We discovered that posts with questions in the caption generated comments, and with using this strategy, we could gain insightful feedback from our audience.







Instagram reel: The degree of engagement we were expecting from our Instagram reel was not achieved by its performance. Because Instagram reels have been more and more popular recently, we had initially expected the reel to be more successful. Reels frequently go viral very quickly since the algorithm tends to promote them more than regular posts.




Good first impression: We discovered that we managed to reach the most followers at the beginning of our campaign. This emphasizes how crucial it is to make a good first impression and make sure the first few posts are compelling and engaging in order to draw the audience in.

Real people: The post with the biggest reach, was part of an experiment we did. We posted an illustration of a task card, and a picture with someone holding a question card. The post with the illustration reaches 24 people, 92% of them were followers. The post with the picture of someone holding the card reached 34 people, 82% of them were followers. This shows that the picture with real people reached more people, and more people that were not actually following us. Therefore, it might be a good idea to use more pictures with real people in the future, to also reach more people that are not following the account.




Facebook: During the project, Facebook has not really been used. An account is created, and the first post is posted, but other than that the focus was mainly on Instagram. Therefore, we missed a lot of opportunities in reaching more people.

This means that the reach goal on Instagram was 100 accounts. We found out that we reached 54 accounts. And 21 of those accounts were not following us. Our goal was to have 100 followers on Instagram, and therefore an average of about 33 likes a post. This goal was not met since we also did not get 100 followers. Future Planning

Because of our reflection on the learning points, we found some points that we would incorporate in the future:
- Asking questions in captions and stories. Stay interactive with our followers and give them some input on our brand.
- Post more pictures with real people. The post that reached the most people was one with real people, therefore we want to use this in the future and post more stuff with real people.
- Focus more on Facebook. Right now, we mostly focused on Instagram, and did not really post anything on Facebook. So, in the future, we want to post more on Facebook so we can reach more people there.
- Be more consistent with posting. We had a period of time where we posted less, so in the future we want to make sure that we really follow our posting agenda and stay consistent with posting.

Services/products

Our product is a family friendly boardgame with fun and educational questions for the ages of 5 between 10. The children can play the game with their parents or with their friends. The game contains a monthly subscription. Every month, a new set of question and task cards will be delivered to your house, this keeps the game new and fresh. Validation of Assumptions

REFLECTION ON TRADEMARK
After the problem interviews we came across multiple different problems that were amongst parents with young children. There were some big problems mentioned but a recurring problem was that parents had a difficulty with entertaining their children without the usage of digital devices. This is something we also thought of ourselves as problem before the interviews and therefore chose to make a question about this problem in the interviews. After the interviews it was something that all the participants agreed on and so we immediately chose to use this as a starting point for creating our trademark. After some discussion the best to execute this, and so on come up with the best solution for this problem, seemed to us to make a boardgame. But no ordinary boardgame. The best way to still keep it entertaining for children without the usage of digital aspects was in our eyes to release every month new cards that they game can played with. So it doesn't get boring for the children. We knew most of the alternatives for the problem were based on keeping the children entertained by giving them something new one in a while. So they keep themselves busy without using digital devices.

REFLECTION AFTER SOLUTION INTERVIEWS
After the first solution interview that I did in the early stages of our project we had to adjust the recommended age for the game to better suit the goal of our intended market. She expressed admiration for the idea but a legitimate concern about the game's suitability for children of the age of her younger one. Her younger child couldn't read the cards yet, so she would have to constantly watch over and help her. A sound effect that would read the cards to the players aloud was another solution that was put out during the interview. We later discovered, however, that some parents preferred their kids to play the game on their own, so they can also learn on how to read. And therefore, in order to maintain the children's sense of independence and self-discovery that the game offers, we chose not to execute this idea.

PIVOTS
First pivot
One of our first pivots, is the change in channels. We decide to not continue with YouTube because it would be to hard to create, produce and publish YouTube content on a frequent basis besides the other social media. So instead of continuing with Facebook we only continued with the other social media.

Second pivot Here we decided to not only focus on parents with young children in Netherlands but also in Europe. We made this decision because of the fact that the product, the game, is easily translatable and so it is easy to distribute it in other countries as it doesn’t include any cultural aspects. Secondly, the problem of rising digitalisation under young children is something that is probably not only occurring in the Netherlands.

Third pivot Later on the website we added the option to sponsored/donate us. So naturally this wasn’t at the first version of the lean canvas. We decided to add this feature because of the extra revenue we could get from it. Also because chameleon has only one product that they can sell makes the company very dependent on that product. But with the revenue from sponsors and donations a little less. Logically this is also something that has been added to the Key Metrics. The social media disappeared at the Key Metrics because the followers and likes on social media weren’t important enough for us in correlation with the selling of the product.

Fourth Pivot The problems that occurred at the problem interviews were as good as solved with the creating of our new board game as seen as outcome by the solution interviews. However after the solution interviews we stumbled upon some new problems that the contestants of these interviews gave us. Logically these problems came with new solutions we later figured out.
